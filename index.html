Strategic Context

The Mission Gap

Platform One's STAR Flight has 4 junior Airmen (ages 19 to 20) writing TypeScript who need constant senior engineering guidance. The current model creates a cascading readiness problem.

⚠

Senior Engineers Diverted

Mission-critical projects lose focus as senior engineers field constant mentorship requests from junior developers learning TypeScript, GitLab CI/CD, and DevSecOps.

⏱

Airmen Blocked Without Support

When senior engineers are unavailable, whether in meetings, on leave, or on other projects, junior Airmen idle. Development velocity drops. Career progression stalls.

💰

Contract Mentorship is Expensive

Leadership is considering an external engineering firm at $15,000 to $25,000 per month per senior engineer. That's $180,000 to $300,000 per year for availability-dependent human mentorship.

✦

Project Wingman: The AI Solution

Deploy 15 specialized AI agents that are always on, never pulled to another project, and cost less than 10% of one human contractor. They surround junior Airmen with expert guidance across every engineering discipline. Powered by Fulcrum, DT's MCP-native multi-agent platform.

The AFSC 1D7X1Z 5-level upgrade requires Airmen to "perform all core tasks without assistance." Every agent is designed around this constraint. AI teaches and the Airman demonstrates. OJT certification stays intact.
